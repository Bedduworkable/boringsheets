Features

Everything you expect from a spreadsheet — nothing you don't.

A focused, native app that respects your files, your machine, and your time.

Real .xlsx

Open and save genuine Excel files — formulas, fonts, borders, merges, charts, conditional formatting and notes all survive the round-trip.

175+ functions

From SUM to XLOOKUP, with cross-sheet references, whole-column ranges and instant incremental recalculation.

Charts & data

Bar, line and pie charts, data validation dropdowns, and Google-Sheets-style column filters with sort and value selection.

Rich formatting

Font family and size, colors, borders, number formats, text wrap, alignment, conditional formatting and freeze panes.

Never goes online

Zero network requests — the app literally can't reach the internet. No account, no cloud, no telemetry. We can't see your sheets, and that's by design.

Native & fast

A canvas-rendered grid with virtual scrolling and worksheet-only zoom. It opens instantly and stays smooth.

Under the hood

Not a wrapper. A real engine.

BoringSheets isn't a thin shell over someone else's library. The spreadsheet engine was written from scratch in TypeScript — a tokenizer, a recursive-descent parser, an evaluator and a cross-sheet dependency graph with incremental recalc and cycle detection.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is BoringSheets really free?

Yes — free for life. There's no subscription, no paywall, no "Pro" tier and no trial that expires. You download it once and use it forever, at no cost.

Can it open my Excel files?

Yes. BoringSheets reads and writes the real .xlsx format, preserving formulas, formatting, charts, merges, conditional formatting and more.

Does it connect to the internet?

No — never. BoringSheets makes zero network requests. It can't upload, sync or "phone home," which means we genuinely have no idea what's in your sheets. Everything happens on your machine, fully offline.

Do you collect my data?

No. Because the app never goes online, your files and their contents can't leave your device through BoringSheets. There's no tracking, no telemetry and no account. See our Privacy Policy.

Is there a Windows version?

BoringSheets is Mac-first. A Windows build is planned and coming soon — choose "Notify me" in the download section.

Why does macOS say the app can't be opened?

The beta build isn't yet code-signed with an Apple Developer ID. On first launch, right-click (or Control-click) the app icon and choose Open, then confirm. This is a one-time step.
