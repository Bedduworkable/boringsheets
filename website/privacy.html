BoringSheets

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 10, 2026

BoringSheets ("we", "us") builds a desktop spreadsheet application and this website. We designed BoringSheets to be local-first and private by default. This policy explains, in plain language, what data is and isn't handled.

The short version

  • The BoringSheets app runs entirely on your device.
  • Your spreadsheets and their contents never leave your computer through the app.
  • There are no accounts, no sign-in, no cloud sync, and no analytics or telemetry in the app.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data — because we don't collect it.

1. The BoringSheets desktop app

BoringSheets is an offline application. When you open, edit, and save spreadsheets:

  • Your files stay on your device. Documents you create or open are read from and written to your local file system only, in the locations you choose.
  • No content is transmitted. The app does not send your spreadsheet data, file names, or formulas to us or to any third party.
  • No tracking or telemetry. The app contains no analytics SDKs, no usage tracking, and no crash-reporting that transmits your data.
  • No account required. You do not register or log in to use the app.

2. This website

The marketing website (boringsheets.app) is intentionally lightweight:

  • No tracking cookies and no advertising or analytics cookies are set by us.
  • Server logs. Our hosting provider may automatically record standard technical information (such as IP address, browser type, and pages requested) for security and reliability. These logs are not used to identify you and are retained only as needed to operate the site.
  • Content delivery network. The site loads an animation library (GSAP) from a third-party CDN (jsDelivr). When your browser requests these files, the CDN may receive your IP address as part of the standard web request. We do not control jsDelivr's logging; see their provider's policy for details.
  • Links and email. If you email us, we receive whatever information you include in your message.

3. Downloads

When you download the app, the file is served by our host or a download/CDN provider, which may log the request as described above. We do not attach identifiers to downloads.

4. Children's privacy

BoringSheets is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children.

5. Your choices and rights

Because the app does not collect personal data, there is generally nothing for us to access, correct, or delete on your behalf. For any data you've sent us directly (for example, an email), you may contact us to request deletion. Depending on where you live, you may have rights under laws such as the GDPR or CCPA; we honor applicable requests.

6. Data security

Your spreadsheet data lives on your own device and is protected by your operating system's security. We encourage you to keep your device and OS up to date and to back up important files.

7. Changes to this policy

If BoringSheets ever adds a feature that changes how data is handled (for example, optional cloud features or opt-in analytics), we will update this policy and the "Last updated" date above, and we will make any such feature clearly opt-in.
